Claim for loss of notice

You’re entitled to claim money if your employer has failed to give you the minimum statutory notice period. The minimum notice is:

  • a weeks notice if you have been employed between a calendar month and less than 2 years
  • a weeks notice for each complete year you’ve worked if you have been employed for 2 years or more (up to a maximum of 12 weeks for 12 years and over)
! Warning Because you’ve been made redundant, it’s very important you apply for employment related benefits such as Universal Credit and New Style Jobseeker’s Allowance.

If you later apply for loss of notice compensation, we’ll ask you about benefits you applied for during your notice period. We deduct them or benefits you were eligible for from your compensation. This includes any:

  • new benefits you were awarded during your notice period
  • increase in existing benefits
  • benefits you could have been awarded during your notice period if you’d applied for them

Do you want to claim compensation for loss of notice?

We will send you an email asking you to complete a second online form after your notice period ends. You will need to complete this form so that we can pay your notice pay.
